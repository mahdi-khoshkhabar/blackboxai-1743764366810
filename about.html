Our History

1976 - Founded in a Garage

Apple Computer Company was founded by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne in Jobs' parents' garage in Los Altos, California.

1984 - Macintosh Introduced

The Macintosh was introduced with a now-famous Super Bowl commercial directed by Ridley Scott, signaling a new era in personal computing.

1998 - iMac Revolution

The colorful iMac G3 marked Apple's resurgence under Steve Jobs' return, combining computing power with consumer-friendly design.

2001 - iPod and Apple Stores

The iPod revolutionized digital music, while the first Apple Stores provided a new way to experience technology.

2007 - iPhone Debut

Steve Jobs introduced the iPhone as "three revolutionary products in one" - a widescreen iPod, phone, and internet communicator.

2020 - Apple Silicon

Apple began transitioning Macs to its own ARM-based processors, marking a new era of performance and efficiency.

Leadership Team

Tim Cook

Chief Executive Officer

Tim Cook is the CEO of Apple and serves on its board of directors. He joined Apple in 1998.

Katherine Adams

Senior Vice President, General Counsel

Katherine Adams oversees all legal matters, corporate governance, and government affairs.

Eddy Cue

Senior Vice President, Services

Eddy Cue leads Apple's services including Apple Music, Apple TV+, and iCloud.

Craig Federighi

Senior Vice President, Software Engineering

Craig Federighi oversees the development of iOS, macOS, and related software.

Deirdre O'Brien

Senior Vice President, Retail + People

Deirdre O'Brien leads Apple's global retail stores and HR functions.

Jeff Williams

Chief Operating Officer

Jeff Williams oversees Apple's entire supply chain, operations, and design teams.
